Position Summary

We're seeking an experienced Irrigation Technician to service and maintain an established book of residential and light commercial irrigation systems. This is a service and maintenance-focused role, not a new-install position; you'll be diagnosing, repairing, and maintaining existing systems across a set territory, with heavy seasonal volume during spring start-ups and fall winterizations.

Key Skills below is a general list — expectations may vary based on project scope.

Installation & Repair (System Components)

  • Install and replace broken/damaged sprinkler heads (rotor, spray, drip)
  • Repair and replace PVC pipe (cutting, gluing, fitting)
  • Work with funny pipe (flexible riser pipe) for head connections
  • Diagnose and repair/replace irrigation controllers (basic programming and troubleshooting)
  • Valve repair and replacement (solenoids, diaphragms, manifolds)

Troubleshooting

  • Diagnose low pressure, zone failures, wiring faults, and valve issues
  • Locate and repair underground wire breaks
  • Identify and correct head coverage/spray pattern issues
  • Read and interpret controller fault codes and wiring diagrams

Seasonal Service

  • Spring start-ups: system activation, pressure testing, zone-by-zone inspection, head adjustment
  • Fall winterization: blow-outs using compressed air, controller shutdown/programming for winter, backflow preventer winterization
  • Manage seasonal scheduling volume across an assigned route

Account Management

  • Maintain and service an existing book of recurring customers/accounts
  • Build relationships with repeat clients to support retention
  • Communicate findings, recommended repairs, and pricing to customers on-site
  • Accurately document work performed, parts used, and system notes per property

Qualifications

  • 2+ years of hands-on irrigation service experience (residential and/or light commercial)
  • Strong working knowledge of PVC solvent welding, poly/funny pipe connections, and common controller brands (Rain Bird, Hunter, Toro, etc.)
  • Experience with spring start-up and fall winterization (compressor blow-out procedure)
  • Backflow preventer testing certification a plus (or willingness to obtain)
  • Irrigation Association Certified Irrigation Technician (CIT) a plus, not required
  • Valid driver's license and clean driving record (will be driving a company vehicle)
  • Physically able to dig, kneel, and work outdoors in varied weather conditions
  • Basic understanding of hydraulics (GPM, PSI, zone sizing) preferred
